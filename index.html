Tanking and the Draft

Do WNBA teams lose on purpose to win later? We follow franchises that chased high draft picks and ask what it cost them — on the court and at the box office.

Attendance and the City

Why do some arenas fill up while others stay quiet? We compare attendance between small cities and big metropolitan areas across nearly three decades.

March Madness stars don't always become WNBA stars. We trace how NCAA tournament success translates — or doesn't — into professional careers.

Awards vs. Reality

Do MVP votes and expert predictions match what actually happens on the court? We test award votes and GM surveys against real performance data.

About This Project

This website was created for Digital Humanities 101 at UCLA. Our project explores the history of women's professional basketball through data from Across the Timeline, a comprehensive archive of WNBA statistics covering player transactions, attendance, drafts, awards, and expert surveys from the league's founding in 1997 to the present.

Rather than treating these numbers as neutral facts, we approach them with a humanistic lens: asking how team strategies like tanking affect a franchise's future, how city size shapes attendance, how college success translates into professional careers, and how well awards and expert opinions reflect players' actual performance.
