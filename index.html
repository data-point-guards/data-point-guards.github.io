Since 1997, every game of the WNBA has left a trail: draft boards, box scores, attendance counts, award ballots, executive surveys. The archive of Across the Timeline holds nearly three decades of it — a complete record of what the league chose to count.

Numbers feel neutral. They are not. Behind every data point is a decision about what deserves measuring, and behind every ranking is a judgment about who deserves remembering. Our team asks one big question of this archive, through six research questions. Keep scrolling — each one appears over the world it describes.

How does the WNBA decide which players and teams matter — in the draft, at the arena, on award ballots, and in the data itself?

Why this matters

Deciding what to count — and what not to count — is a human choice with consequences, as our data critique explores. A league's data is its memory, and memory is power: it decides which seasons get retold and which quietly disappear. This project reads the WNBA's memory closely, and asks what it reveals — and hides.
