The Team

About This Project

This website was created for Digital Humanities 101 at UCLA. Our project explores the history of women’s professional basketball through data from Across the Timeline, a comprehensive archive of WNBA statistics covering player transactions, attendance, drafts, awards, and expert surveys from the league’s founding in 1997 to the present.

Rather than treating these numbers as neutral facts, we approach them with a humanistic lens: following a player’s journey — from college, through the draft, to professional recognition — and asking at each step what the data reveals, and what it hides. The site’s six research chapters each pursue one of the research questions our team developed together, all asking one bigger question: how does the WNBA decide which players and teams matter?
